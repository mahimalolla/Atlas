Company: TerraStack
Stage: Series C
Sector: Infrastructure Software

Problem:
TerraStack had three machine learning pilots but no reliable path to production. Models were trained in notebooks, monitoring was inconsistent, and engineering teams disagreed on who owned deployment.

Root Cause:
The company treated ML as experimentation rather than a production software capability. There was no shared model registry, evaluation process, or deployment ownership model.

Actions Taken:
The AI working group created a lightweight MLOps standard. The first version included a shared model registry, evaluation checklist, model owner assignment, rollback plan, and observability dashboard for latency, cost, and quality metrics.

Outcome:
Two models moved from pilot to production within one quarter. The company avoided building a large platform team and instead standardized the minimum process needed for safe deployment.

Lesson:
MLOps maturity should match the number and criticality of models in production. Early-stage teams should standardize evaluation, ownership, and monitoring before investing in a heavy internal platform.

Tags:
MLOps, production AI, model deployment, observability, infrastructure software, Series C
